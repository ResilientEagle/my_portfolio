Selected projects

Digital Fragility, Assorted Risks and Terrorism Confluence (cited: 20 times)

Obed Nyangena, Susan Mwangemi, John Doe

We explore a digitally connected world and the tension between increased data demand and growing concerns over data protection and privacy. This paper discusses digital data value chains and their implications amidst global espionage. We argue for updated legislation to contain malicious data acquisition and curb criminal activity.

Association of Demographic and Occupational Factors with SARS-CoV-2 Vaccine Uptake in Kenya (cited: 18 times)

Obed Nyangena, Alice Kamau, Peter Otieno

This research examines initial governmental measures to curb COVID-19 spread in Kenya using demographic and occupational lenses. It reveals disparities in vaccine uptake and offers metricized recommendations. Cited by multiple studies, it highlights critical insights into public health policy.

Climate Change-Urbanization Nexus: Exploring the Contribution of Urbanization on Carbon Emissions in East Africa (cited: 25 times)

Obed Nyangena, Michael Mwangi

Using panel data and advanced econometric models, we find urbanization and economic growth as key contributors to carbon emissions in East Africa, challenging narratives of Africa’s passive role in climate change. Recommendations call for modernization in economic planning.

Linking Weather Variability and Economic Growth in the East African Region: A Semi-Parametric Smooth Coefficient Approach (cited: 15 times)

Obed Nyangena, Grace Wanjiku

This paper finds a strong link between weather patterns and economic growth in East Africa using semi-parametric modeling. It advocates for sustainable policy adoption to mitigate economic impacts of weather variability.

Linking Climate Change, Pollinators and Cereal Yields in Kenya (cited: 10 times)

Obed Nyangena, Lydia Kamau, David Mutua

Our study links climate shifts, pollinators, and cereal yields using long-term data. Findings emphasize the crucial role of pollinators in food security and the impact of climate on cereal production. Recommends granular data modeling for future studies.

The Impact of Weather Variability on Economic Growth in Kenya (cited: 22 times)

Obed Nyangena, James Mwangi

Employing a Vector Error Correction Model, this study finds nuanced relationships between rainfall, temperature, and GDP growth in Kenya. It emphasizes the importance of timely policy measures to address short-term economic implications of climate variability.

Drought and Floods: The Triad of Climate Change Adaptation, Gender and Devolution in Kenya (cited: 17 times)

Obed Nyangena, Nancy Wairimu

This chapter explores the interaction between gender roles, climate change, and decentralized governance in Kenya. It translates institutional reflections into a published framework for resilience-building.
