Obed Nyangena & Mavia Imbali

This article provides a discursive relationship between digital leakages and gains as economies, individuals and groups jockey to advance their objectives. Data protection and privacy frameworks reveal inadequacy in catching up with new technological improvements as risks soar.

Association of Demographic and Occupational Factors with SARS-CoV-2 Vaccine Uptake in Kenya (cited: 18 times)

Linking Weather Variability and Economic Growth in the East African Region: A Semi-Parametric Smooth Coefficient Approach (cited: 15 times)

Obed Nyangena, Victor Kidake & Benson S. Igesa

we dissect inaccuracies around the climate change narratives in Africa using panel data for selected East African countries and conduct analysis on the urbanization-carbon emissions models that determine the presence of an Environmental Kuznets Curve hypothesis. We execute parametric and semi-parametric fixed-effects models and compare results, which reveal that urbanization and economic growth are responsible for continued environmental deterioration in the region. We conclude that contrary to the popular perception that East Africa, like the rest of the continent, is only a victim than a contributor of climate change, it is an important emitter of carbon dioxide mainly because of urbanization and recommend infusion of extra-modernization designs in economic planning to attenuate this startling problem.

Linking Climate Change, Pollinators and Cereal Yields in Kenya (cited: 10 times)

Obed Nyangena, Victor Kidake Senelwa, Rachel Ngesa

Our study links climate shifts, pollinators, and cereal yields using long-term data. Findings emphasize the crucial role of pollinators in food security and the impact of climate on cereal production. Recommends granular data modeling for future studies.

The Impact of Weather Variability on Economic Growth in Kenya (cited: 22 times)

Obed Nyangena, James Mwangi

Employing a Vector Error Correction Model, this study finds nuanced relationships between rainfall, temperature, and GDP growth in Kenya. It emphasizes the importance of timely policy measures to address short-term economic implications of climate variability.

Obed Nyangena, Nancy Wairimu

This chapter explores the interaction between gender roles, climate change, and decentralized governance in Kenya. It translates institutional reflections into a published framework for resilience-building.
